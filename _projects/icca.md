Intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (iCCA) is an aggressive biliary tract cancer with poor prognosis and limited therapeutic options. This project applies **multiregion whole-exome sequencing (WES)** and **spatial transcriptomics** to characterize the intratumoral heterogeneity and subclonal evolutionary dynamics of iCCA.

### Aims

- Reconstruct tumor phylogenies from multiregion WES to define the temporal order of driver mutations
- Quantify spatial variation in copy number alterations (SCNAs) and clonal composition across tumor regions
- Integrate spatial transcriptomics to map immune microenvironment heterogeneity in relation to tumor clones
- Identify genomic features associated with metastasis and clinical outcomes
